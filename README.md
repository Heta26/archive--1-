# Background
This project marks my first full SQL analysis, inspired by a simple but practical question: Which products truly drive sales across different regions, and what stood out in 2024? Working with an e‑commerce dataset, I wanted to understand not just totals, but patterns—what sells where, which products bring in the most profit, and how pricing behaves across categories.
To explore these questions, I relied on a set of tools that supported every step of the workflow:
-	SQL — The core language I used to query, clean, and analyze the data.
-	PostgreSQL — A stable and flexible database engine that handled the dataset efficiently.
- 	Visual Studio Code — My working environment for managing the database and running SQL scripts.
- 	Git and GitHub — Essential for version control, sharing my queries, and keeping the project organized.
This combination allowed me to build a structured, transparent analysis from scratch.

# Objectives
My analysis focused on answering key business‑driven questions:
-	What is the average sales of accessories in the North region?
-	What is the average sales across all regions?
- 	What is the total profit generated in 2024?
-	Which product made the highest profit in 2024?
- 	What is the unit price of all products in the category column?
These questions guided the structure of my queries and helped me uncover meaningful insights from the dataset.

# Analysis Approach
Each SQL query targeted a specific aspect of the sales data. Through filtering, grouping, and aggregations, I was able to:
-	Compute unit prices by dividing total sales by total quantity sold.
- 	Identify the highest‑profit products both in 2024 and overall.
- 	Compare regional performance and calculate average sales.
- 	Determine the total profit generated during the year.
This step‑by‑step approach helped me understand not just the numbers, but the story behind them—how different products performed, how regions varied, and where the business earned the most value.

# Closing Thoughts
The analysis revealed several clear insights:
-	The business generated 4,234,592.67 in total profit in 2024.
- 	Monitor was the highest‑profit product for the year.
-	Camera had the highest overall profit, with a unit price of 655.
-	Calculating unit prices across categories helped highlight pricing differences and product value.
This project strengthened my SQL foundation and showed me how much insight can be extracted from raw data with the right questions and tools.
